About us

NZ Final Drives provides New Zealanders with affordable high quality after-market final drives. We're proud to be partnered with AUS Final Drives, who have been doing business in Australia since 2015.

By keeping our overheads to a minimum and leveraging off our international partners, we're able to generate more sales, resulting in greater savings for our customers. We only sell brand-new final drives, which come included with a 2-year unlimited-hour warranty.

And if that wasn't enough, our drives are carefully packed and couriered all across New Zealand, with free delivery to the Wellington region, and optional pickup from Johnsonville.

FAQ

Should I buy one drive or two?

While you can mix old and new drives, we recommend you buy two, especially if you use your excavator frequently. Buying two new drives guarantees equal performance and straight driving.

If I connect the hoses to the drive incorrectly, will I damage it?

YES! Typically you will have four hoses connecting each drive to your excavator (unless it’s a 1-speed machine, in which case you will have three). The two larger hoses are your Forward and Reverse. A smaller hose will be your 2-speed connection, and there will also be a case drain hose (oil return).

Typically the case drain hose can be connected to an upper or lower port on the drive depending on which side the drive is being installed. We provide a connection diagram in the box with each drive we sell.

If you connect the hoses wrong you risk instantly destroying the drive when it’s put under load.

Is it cheaper to rebuild a drive or buy a new one from you?

The cost of new aftermarket final drives has reduced significantly in recent years, thanks to high-volume production systems and improved tooling technology. That being said, there are times when rebuilding drives is the more practical option, especially if your machine has an unusual drive.

Also note that not all new drives are the same. That's why we provide a 2-year unlimited hour warranty for all of our drives. You won’t get that with a rebuilt drive!

What oil does the drive use and how often should I replace it?

Use gear oil SAE80/90W, and change it every 250 hours. The 1 litre and 2.5 litre packs have pull out snorkels making it easy to pour into your drive and are readily available from Repco and Supercheap Auto. A 1 litre pack will be enough for most drives up to 5-6 tonnes.

What is the typical freight cost for one of your drives?

Our drives vary considerably in size and weight, from under 20 kg to over 500 kg, so the cost will vary accordingly. All drives are pre-packed in a forklift pallet. You can pick up from us directly and we can load into your vehicle, or we can provide a competitive freight quote to your address.
